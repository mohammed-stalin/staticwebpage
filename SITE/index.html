Morocco stuns Belgium in historic World Cup win

The Qatari players might be jealous. On Sunday, November 27, during their victory against Belgium (2-0), the Moroccan team played as if they were at home. The Al Thumama stadium may be located in the southern zone of Doha, but the round venue could just as well have been erected south of Marrakech or Tangier. Moroccan supporters decided to play their role as the twelfth man to the fullest, or even that of a thirteenth or fourteenth man.

Every Belgian possession was booed, every offensive, every defensive gesture from Morocco was encouraged. A hostile atmosphere for Belgium's Eden Hazard, the world's second-ranked nation according to FIFA. Thanks to this victory, the Atlas Lions took the lead in Group F, with four points, pending the result of the other match between Croatia and Canada.

Winners of their first match (1-0 against Canada), the Belgians tried to resist the atmosphere. Led by Eden Hazard or Kevin De Bruyne in the first half, they most often calmed this wind coming from the Atlas by confiscating the ball. The Moroccans, for their part, ran a lot and fought like demons against the Red Devils. But with such an atmosphere, nothing surprising.

Folded back in their camp, the Atlas Lions sometimes came out of it in a sharp manner. It was during stoppage time that a free kick from Zyech set the stadium on fire. His shot surprised Courtois. The goal was rightly disallowed for an offside by Romain Saïss, who did not touch the ball but did obstruct the Real Madrid goalkeeper's field of vision.

And the Moroccans were more enterprising in the second half. Despite an attempt by Eden Hazard, pushed back by Mohamedi (52nd), they positioned themselves much higher, managing to protect themselves from Belgian counterattacks. The Angers' Sofiane Boufal even thought of opening the scoring: his curled shot grazed Courtois' left post (59th). At the hour mark, the two teams then went blow for blow. The Belgian, Dries Mertens, new entrant, forced Mohamedi to make an effective save (65th).

But it was the choices of coach Walid Regragui that made the difference. One of the substitutes, Abdelhamid Sabiri, fooled the best goalkeeper in the world. Off-center on the left side, in an impossible angle, Sabiri scored directly, against a helpless Thibaut Courtois (73rd, 1-0). The break goal - and the Belgian chaos - was the work of another substitute, Toulouse's Zakaria Aboukhlal who converted in stoppage time an offering from Zyech (92nd, 2-0).

On Thursday, December 1, Achraf Hakimi's teammates will have to validate their participation in the round of 16 against Canada. A performance they have only achieved once, during the 1986 World Cup in Mexico. In 1998, despite 4 points, they had failed by a whisker behind Norway, beaten by one point after a controversial victory by the Norwegians against Brazil.

Argentina breathes again after crucial Mexico win

So, when the draw seemed to be on the horizon, Messi came out of his box, and single-handedly transformed the face of an Argentina that was drastically more mobile and finally recognizable in this World Cup after opening the scoring.

"In the second half, we started to dominate in midfield and then they saw what happened: The number 10 always decides the match, the 10 decides he's the best," Argentina coach Lionel Scaloni said after the match. "Behind him there's a group that supports him and who know his importance. It's exciting at all levels."

In serious trouble after their opening defeat to Saudi Arabia (1-2), Argentina didn't even have time to savor, as they will need to beat Poland on Wednesday (8pm) to secure a place in the last 16. More than the victory, it's a regained confidence that seems to be calming the Argentine dressing room.

"We showed that we have character and that we are a great group," said Emiliano "Dibu" Martinez. "Messi is the best player in the world and he will always have a chance. Playing with him is always easier." Messi, as the great generational leader that he is, may well have unlocked Argentine minds often shaken by telluric pressure. Just like a certain Diego did in his time.

Morata and Kramaric join World Cup top scorers

Spain's Álvaro Morata and Croatia's Andrej Kramaric joined the group of players with two goals in the 2022 World Cup on Sunday, one length behind France's Kylian Mbappé and Ecuador's Enner Valencia, who each have three goals. Morata, a striker at Atlético Madrid, opened the scoring against Germany (1-1), eight minutes after coming on (62nd), and Kramaric, who plays for Hoffenheim in Germany, scored a brace against Canada (4-1).
